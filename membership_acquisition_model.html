Membership Acquisition Model

Can we predict and explain who is most likely to become a Brooklyn Museum Member?

This project used an imbalanced classification model to predict which museum visitors were most likely to respond to a targeted membership campaign. The model was trained on historical engagement, visitation patterns, ticketing behavior, and proximity to the museum. Feature importance and calibration were used to improve model interpretability and lift-based targeting.

The final model outperformed random mailing by a significant margin and has been proposed for integration into the museum’s marketing workflow.

The Brooklyn Museum is one year into its customer relationship management (CRM) journey with Tessitura, a platform that has begun to centralize and enrich its understanding of visitor engagement. As the institution builds a more unified data infrastructure, it has also expressed a strategic interest in improving the efficiency of its membership acquisition efforts—particularly in converting ticket buyers and event attendees into long-term members. This model was developed to support that goal: using behavioral, geographic, and transactional data, it aims to identify high-potential prospects and provide a data-informed foundation for targeted outreach campaigns.
